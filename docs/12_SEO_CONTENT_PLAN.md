# SEO and Content Plan

## 1. Purpose

The website must support two organic acquisition categories:

- guests looking for cabins;
- property owners looking for management services.

Guest intent is the primary volume opportunity. Owner intent is lower volume but commercially valuable.

## 2. Guest search intent

Initial themes:

- cabin rentals Gatlinburg;
- cabin rentals Pigeon Forge;
- cabin rentals Sevierville;
- cabins with hot tubs;
- mountain view cabins;
- pet-friendly cabins;
- cabins for groups or families.

Do not create a landing page for every possible combination at launch.

## 3. Property owner intent

Initial themes:

- property management Gatlinburg;
- Airbnb management Gatlinburg;
- vacation rental management Pigeon Forge;
- cabin property management Sevierville;
- short-term rental cleaning and management;
- direct booking distribution as part of management.

The lean launch may use one strong property-management page rather than three thin town variants.

## 4. Page priority

### Tier 1 launch pages

- Homepage.
- All cabins.
- Three destination pages.
- Property detail pages.
- Property management.
- Rental analysis/consultation.
- About and Contact.

### Tier 2 launch or early follow-up

- Hot tub cabins.
- Mountain view cabins.
- Pet-friendly cabins.
- Guest FAQ.
- One useful area guide.

### Later content programme

- attraction and planning guides;
- event content;
- detailed owner service pages;
- owner case studies;
- market reports;
- repeat guest content;
- comparison and seasonal pages.

## 5. Content rules

- Each indexable page must have a distinct purpose.
- Destination content should help a guest plan and choose, not only repeat keywords.
- Feature content should explain the experience and practical considerations.
- Property pages should use authorized, property-specific content.
- Owner pages should explain service, proof, process, and next step.
- Avoid unsupported earnings, ranking, occupancy, or revenue guarantees.
- Avoid automatically generating thin pages for every filter combination.
- Avoid copied destination or property descriptions.

## 6. On-page requirements

Each indexable page needs:

- clear primary heading;
- useful title and description;
- canonical URL;
- contextual internal links;
- relevant image with dimensions and alt treatment;
- appropriate structured data when accurate;
- useful body content;
- an audience-specific conversion action.

## 7. Property page SEO

Property pages should include:

- unique property name;
- town and region context;
- bedrooms, bathrooms, and capacity;
- distinguishing amenities;
- original description;
- approved images;
- breadcrumbs;
- stable canonical slug;
- noindex behaviour for demo or incomplete listings;
- archived-property and redirect policy.

Do not expose exact address if the business does not want it public before booking.

## 8. Search and canonical strategy

- `/cabins` is the principal results route.
- Query parameters represent user state.
- Arbitrary filter combinations should normally be non-canonical or canonical to the base intentional landing route.
- Dedicated destination and feature pages are the indexable organic routes.
- Pagination/infinite loading behaviour should have an intentional crawl strategy if inventory grows.

## 9. Internal linking

Homepage should link to:

- three destinations;
- approved features;
- featured properties;
- property management.

Destination pages should link to:

- matching properties;
- relevant features;
- other nearby towns;
- useful guides when available.

Property pages should link to:

- destination parent;
- selected feature pages;
- related properties;
- contact/enquiry.

Owner pages should link to:

- rental analysis;
- About;
- Contact;
- future local owner pages or case studies.

## 10. Content inputs required from client

- Brand positioning.
- Business name and contact details.
- Service list.
- Cleaning and operations proof.
- Owner process.
- Owner CTA and response promise.
- Approved property content.
- Destination knowledge.
- Testimonials with permission.
- Legal and privacy text.

## 11. Analytics and conversion events

Guest:

- organic landing page viewed;
- search submitted;
- filter applied;
- property viewed;
- enquiry started/submitted;
- external booking clicked;
- phone clicked.

Owner:

- property management page viewed;
- rental analysis started/submitted;
- phone/email clicked;
- source town/page and campaign.

Do not send personal lead values into analytics.

## 12. Ranking expectations

The build can deliver technical and content foundations. It cannot guarantee ranking positions or traffic volume.

Organic growth will also depend on:

- domain history and authority;
- content quality and consistency;
- competition;
- links and local citations;
- real property inventory;
- reviews and business reputation;
- ongoing optimization;
- time.

Treat any quoted keyword volume as an input to verify before commercial forecasting.
