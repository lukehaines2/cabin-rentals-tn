# Client Context and Voice Note Synthesis

## 1. Original discovery questions

The project lead asked the client to explain:

1. What are we building?
2. What do we want to achieve?
3. What do we want to achieve in relation to the reference site?
4. What is in the current scope?
5. What would be ideal later or in a second iteration?
6. What market, budget, time, or other constraints apply?

## 2. Cleaned transcript: product and market

The client explained that the business already has a cleaning company in the area and wants to start offering property management.

The desired website should allow a guest to search according to preferred vacation dates and property filters, such as hot tubs, mountain views, and other property features. The results should contain properties taken on as property management clients, and guests should eventually be able to find and book those properties.

The client described a target state in which the website synchronizes with booking and property management systems such as Airbnb, Vrbo, and Booking.com so that the dates shown on the website remain accurate.

The client identified Gatlinburg, Pigeon Forge, and Sevierville in Sevier County, Tennessee as the principal market.

The only explicit project constraint mentioned was budget.

## 3. Cleaned transcript: two-sided website

The client stated that the business idea does not need market validation because many companies already operate in this way.

The website needs:

- a guest-facing interface for discovering cabins;
- property-owner-facing pages explaining why owners should choose the company for property management.

The client wants the site to rank for both categories of search:

- guest queries such as cabin rentals in Gatlinburg;
- owner queries such as property management company in Gatlinburg or Sevierville.

Guest searches are expected to have much higher volume and should be the main structure and priority of the website. The owner acquisition hook remains essential because the business needs managed properties to create inventory.

## 4. Cleaned transcript: property management value proposition

The client described the service as similar to hiring someone to manage an Airbnb. The property manager would handle guest communication, listing optimization, and efforts to improve performance on Airbnb and other booking platforms.

The direct website is an additional distribution service offered to property owners as part of the broader management package.

The client believes some travellers who search for cabin rentals will book through Airbnb or Booking.com, while others will prefer to book directly on a website they trust. Direct bookings are intended to generate incremental revenue for property owners and demonstrate the management company's value.

Because the management company expects to earn a share of managed-property revenue, more revenue for owners also benefits the company.

## 5. Consolidated business interpretation

This is a full-service property management business with a direct website as one distribution and acquisition channel.

The website supports two connected funnels:

### Guest demand funnel

```text
Search engine or direct visit
-> destination or feature landing page
-> property search
-> property detail
-> enquiry or booking handoff in Phase 1
-> direct booking in a later phase
```

### Owner supply funnel

```text
Search engine or referral
-> property management page
-> service and proof content
-> rental analysis or consultation form
-> sales follow-up
-> management agreement
-> property onboarding
-> property added to guest inventory
```

The business flywheel is:

```text
More owner clients
-> more managed cabins
-> stronger guest proposition
-> more bookings
-> stronger owner proof
-> more owner clients
```

## 6. Confirmed context

The following statements are confirmed by the client transcripts:

- The business already operates a cleaning company in the area.
- It intends to expand into short-term rental property management.
- It wants to manage guest communication and listing performance.
- The focus market is Gatlinburg, Pigeon Forge, and Sevierville.
- The public site must serve guests and property owners.
- Guest acquisition is the higher-priority traffic opportunity.
- Only properties taken on as management clients should appear.
- The direct website is intended to generate extra owner revenue.
- The management company expects to benefit financially from increased managed-property revenue.
- Live synchronized dates and direct booking are part of the long-term vision.
- Budget is the principal stated constraint.

## 7. Strongly inferred context

These conclusions are strongly supported but still require explicit business approval:

- The site should present one coherent hospitality brand, not a directory of unrelated owners.
- New managed properties should eventually be standardized on one company PMS/channel manager.
- The owner-facing experience is initially lead generation, not a self-service owner portal.
- Direct booking attribution will eventually be needed to demonstrate owner value.
- The management company should own or coordinate the guest support process.
- The first release can launch before live booking integration if its claims are clear.

## 8. Unresolved business questions

- What exact action should a guest complete in the first release?
- Are there already signed launch properties?
- Who owns or licenses the initial property images and descriptions?
- What is the approved launch budget?
- What is the target launch date?
- Is a property management system already selected?
- Is a hosted third-party booking handoff acceptable?
- Who will be merchant of record in the future?
- Who approves booking, privacy, and management-service claims?
- Does the business have a final name, brand, domain, and contact details?

## 9. Terminology to use

Use:

- guest;
- property owner;
- staff;
- managed property;
- property management service;
- direct booking channel.

Avoid using `client` without qualification because it may refer to either a guest or a property owner.
