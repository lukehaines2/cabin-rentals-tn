# Estimate, Time, Cost, and Commercial Framework

## 1. Estimate the simple Phase 1, not the final platform

The current Phase 1 is a custom guest discovery and property-owner acquisition website.

Do not include future PMS, live availability, Stripe, reservations, or channel synchronization in the Phase 1 fixed estimate.

## 2. Effort range

A reasonable internal range for a solo mid-level developer using AI-assisted delivery is:

- base delivery: 35 to 45 working days;
- contingency: 15 to 20 percent;
- quote basis: approximately 40 to 54 working days;
- calendar duration: approximately 8 to 12 weeks, depending on focus, approvals, and content readiness.

A broader custom design, more content, or more revision cycles can push this higher.

## 3. Example effort breakdown

| Workstream | Likely days |
|---|---:|
| Scope lock and setup | 3 |
| UX and visual foundation | 5 |
| Payload schema and seed | 5 |
| Guest vertical slice | 6 |
| Full search and property experience | 9 |
| Owner funnel and destination templates | 6 |
| SEO, analytics, accessibility, performance | 5 |
| Content, QA, launch, handover | 7 |
| Base total | 46 |

For a very lean launch, reduce optional pages and visual customization rather than reducing QA or scope clarity.

## 4. Three-point estimation

For each major item, estimate:

- best case;
- likely case;
- difficult case.

Weighted estimate:

```text
(best + 4 * likely + difficult) / 6
```

Then add:

- project management and client communication;
- content-loading allowance;
- contingency.

## 5. Cost formula

```text
fixed price = estimated days * day rate * contingency factor
```

Examples at a 15 percent contingency:

| Quote days before contingency | Day rate | Approximate price |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 40 | GBP 400 | GBP 18,400 |
| 45 | GBP 450 | GBP 23,288 |
| 50 | GBP 450 | GBP 25,875 |
| 50 | GBP 500 | GBP 28,750 |

Use the freelancer's sustainable rate and local contract currency. Do not price purely according to the number of keystrokes saved by AI.

## 6. Suggested client options

### Lean launch

Indicative scope:

- guest homepage;
- basic search and filters;
- ten properties;
- property detail template;
- three destination pages;
- one property-management page;
- owner lead form;
- enquiry/external booking;
- basic SEO and analytics;
- restrained custom design;
- one formal revision round.

Illustrative range:

- approximately 7 to 10 weeks;
- approximately GBP 15,000 to GBP 18,000, depending on rate and content readiness.

### Recommended Phase 1

Indicative scope:

- stronger custom design;
- polished mobile and desktop search;
- robust CMS workflow;
- broader owner content;
- feature pages;
- stronger testing, accessibility, performance, and analytics;
- two revision rounds.

Illustrative range:

- approximately 10 to 14 weeks;
- approximately GBP 23,000 to GBP 27,000, depending on rate and final scope.

The project lead currently wants the simplest credible Phase 1, so start with the lean scope and preserve future architecture.

## 7. Paid definition sprint

Before a fixed client quote, offer a 3 to 5 day paid definition sprint that produces:

- page map;
- wireframes for primary journeys;
- property data schema;
- filter list;
- content responsibility matrix;
- technical architecture;
- acceptance criteria;
- final schedule and fixed price.

This converts uncertainty into a priced plan rather than hiding risk inside an optimistic estimate.

## 8. Assumptions that protect the estimate

State that the estimate assumes:

- approximately ten properties;
- client-supplied authorized content;
- one language;
- no live availability;
- no PMS or OTA integration;
- no payment;
- no accounts;
- no owner dashboard;
- one CMS;
- a limited page/template count;
- one decision-maker;
- feedback within agreed time;
- limited revision rounds;
- third-party subscriptions paid by client;
- legal copy supplied or approved by client.

## 9. Change control

Treat these as separate estimates:

- new unique templates;
- additional filter categories;
- custom map search;
- favourites;
- blog/editorial programme;
- CRM integration;
- custom staff dashboard;
- live PMS availability;
- hosted booking engine;
- Stripe and reservation creation;
- owner accounts/reporting.

## 10. Suggested milestone payments

Example:

- 25 percent at booking/kickoff;
- 25 percent after UX direction approval;
- 30 percent after working staging delivery;
- 20 percent after final QA and launch readiness.

Do not make most of the payment dependent on a launch date that can be delayed by client content or access.

## 11. Client-facing explanation

Suggested plain-language summary:

> Phase 1 creates the public website and the two acquisition journeys. Guests will be able to search and filter an initial set of cabins, view detailed property pages, and submit an enquiry or continue to an approved booking route. Property owners will be able to understand the management service and request a consultation. The website will use a central content system so staff can manage properties and pages. Live availability, direct payment, and Airbnb/Vrbo synchronization are a later integration phase because they depend on selecting and proving the property-management platform.

## 12. AI positioning

Use AI to accelerate research, implementation, testing, and documentation, but personally review and own the result.

Do not promise an unrealistic discount because AI is involved. Requirements, content, design decisions, browser testing, accessibility, deployment, and commercial responsibility remain real work.
